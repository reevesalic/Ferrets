Ferret illnesses

I've had a total of 25 ferrets since 2006. The average age of a ferret is between 5-7 years.
This is a list of the common illnesses in ferrets.
This is not a comprehensive list of illnesses. This is just the most common issues.

Illnesses:

  • Infection (that can lead to organ failure and even death if untreated)
  • Blockages (that includes hairballs, foam, rubber, etc)
  • Insulinoma (low blood sugar)
  • Adrenal disease
  • Tumors or Cancer (leukemia and lymphoma are the most common)
  • Cysts (especially on kidneys)
  • Enlarged spleen
  • Diarrhea (specifically Irritable bowel syndrome)
  • How to recognize possible symptoms of heart disease in your ferret.

All of the items on this list will be fatal if not treated. Not all conditions listed are treatable but there are
options to slow down the progressive diseases. Vet care is critial for ferrets.
