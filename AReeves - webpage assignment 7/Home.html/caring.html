Caring for your ferret

Ferrets require time, patience, understanding, ferret proofing, education, proper care and love.

Ferrets are very social animals. Most of them love to cuddle with humans or other ferrets.
They are very smart and curious. You have to ferret proof your house since they can fit into small places.
If their head can fit through an opening, they flatten out their body and can squeeze through.
They require a lot of care. You have to cut their nails every 2 weeks, clean their ears and brush their teeth.
Since ferrets are very curious and smart they require constant stimulation. They become bored very quickly
and get depressed and will get into trouble if they do not have enough play time.

Ferrets can be nippy when they are young but often grow out of their nippy stage.
When ferrets are babies they are called kits. When ferrets become 5 years of age, they should be vetted
more frequently as they can develop quite a few medical issues.
Ferrets are carnivores and need plenty of protein. Some people feed them raw meat. It is believed ferrets
do not get salmonella poisoning because their digestive tract is so small, they dispose of their food before it
affects them. Some people feed their ferrets high quality kibble. The key is looking at the ingredients
if it has peas or anything other than meat in the first 8 ingredients it is best to pass.
Cat kibble is the best to feed your ferret but it is important to look at the ingredients.
